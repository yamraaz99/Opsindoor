OPERATION SINDOOR

84 Hours. Precision. Retribution.

April 22, 2025

The Pahalgam Massacre

In a barbaric act, Pakistan-based terrorists from "The Resistance Front" (TRF) murder 26 innocent tourists in Pahalgam after identifying them by religion. The attack shocks the nation and serves as the catalyst for India's decisive military response.

Jammu & Kashmir, Pahalgam
April 23, 2025

The Diplomatic Strike

India's Cabinet Committee on Security convenes an emergency meeting. They immediately suspend the Indus Water Treaty, close a key border post, and take multiple diplomatic actions against Pakistan, signaling that this time, the response will be different.

Attari Border & Indus River
May 7, 2025 (Early Morning)

Operation Sindoor Begins

Indian forces launch 'Operation Sindoor', a series of precise, non-escalatory strikes. In just 22 minutes, they target nine key terrorist infrastructure sites deep inside Pakistan and Pakistan-occupied Kashmir without crossing the Line of Control.

Duration

May 7-8, 2025 (Overnight)

Pakistan Retaliates

Considering the strikes on terrorists as an attack on itself, Pakistan launches retaliatory drone and missile attacks. They target over a dozen Indian military installations across the Northern and Western fronts, escalating the conflict.

Srinagar, Pathankot, Bhuj
May 8, 2025 (Morning)

The AD Systems Respond

Having successfully neutralized Pakistan's initial assault with a robust air defence grid, Indian forces conduct a swift counter-strike. They target and neutralize a key Pakistani Air Defence system in Lahore.

Lahore, Pakistan
May 8-9, 2025 (Overnight)

The Drone Swarm

Pakistan dramatically escalates with a massive aerial intrusion, launching 300-400 drones along the entire border from Leh to Sir Creek. India's multi-layered defence systems engage and destroy numerous threats, gathering crucial intelligence from the wreckage.

Threat Level

300-400 Drones

May 9-10, 2025 (Overnight)

The Decisive Response

Following a warning from the U.S. of a massive impending attack, India unleashes a devastating counter-offensive. Indian missiles strike military targets across Pakistan with overwhelming force, crippling key airbases and infrastructure.

Skardu, Sargodha, Jacobabad
May 10, 2025 (1535 Hours)

The Call for De-escalation

Unable to endure further assault, Pakistan's Director General of Military Operations (DGMO) makes a direct call to his Indian counterpart. He formally requests a cessation of all firing and military activities.

New Delhi ↔ Islamabad
May 10, 2025 (1700 Hours)

The Guns Fall Silent

A mutual understanding to halt all military action on land, air, and sea comes into effect. While the active conflict is paused, India makes it clear that its broader operation against terror is not over.

Ceasefire Active
July 29, 2025

A New Doctrine

Prime Minister Modi addresses the Indian Parliament, declaring that Operation Sindoor has established a new doctrine against terror. He affirms that the operation remains active and any future aggression will be met with a formidable retaliation.

India's Doctrine of Punitive Deterrence

THE DOCTRINE SHIFT

A New Normal

Operation Sindoor was India's strategic response to the barbaric April 22nd Pahalgam Attack, where terrorists murdered 26 civilians. The operation's core purpose was to shift from a policy of strategic restraint to one of active Punitive Deterrence.

By striking deep into Pakistan at terror hubs like Bahawalpur and Muridke, India demonstrated its resolve to impose a heavy cost for any future attacks, reject nuclear blackmail, and hold state sponsors of terror directly accountable for their actions.

"Operation Sindoor has established a new doctrine against terror. The operation remains active and any future aggression will be met with formidable retaliation."

— Prime Minister Narendra Modi, July 29, 2025
