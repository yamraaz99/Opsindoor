Operation Sindoor

An anatomy of the 2025 Indo-Pakistani conflict, from provocation to strategic cessation.

The Catalyst

A Line Crossed

On April 22, 2025, a barbaric terror attack in Pahalgam claimed 26 innocent lives. This was not just an attack, but a "barbarity that shocked the entire country and the world."

The Resolve

Following a Cabinet Committee of Security (CCS) meeting on April 23rd, the decision was made. India would send a "clear, strong and resolute message."

"India's stand is very clear... Terror and talks cannot go together."
— Prime Minister Narendra Modi

The Chronology

The 19-Day Escalation

From the initial strike to the final ceasefire, a precise sequence of events unfolded, demonstrating a new doctrine of calibrated response.

Phase 1: The Response

May 6-7: Operation Launched

Indian Armed Forces conduct precision strikes against nine terrorist infrastructure sites. The Ministry of Defence states the action is "focused, measured and non-escalatory," targeting only terror hideouts.

Phase 2: Retaliation

May 7-8: Failed Escalation

Pakistan launches a "failed unprovoked drone and missile attack" targeting over a dozen Indian military installations. Concurrently, Indian forces neutralize Pakistani Air Defence Radars, including one at Lahore.

Phase 3: The Brink

May 9: The 1,000-Missile Attack

Following a warning from the US Vice President, Pakistan attempts a major offensive with ~1,000 missiles and drones. India's integrated air defence "neutralized all of them mid-air."

Phase 4: The Counterstrike

May 9-10: Decisive Response

In a powerful counter, "Indian missiles struck across Pakistan with such intensity that it surpassed their imagination." Targets include major airbases like Skardu, Sargodha, and Jacobabad.

Phase 5: Cessation

May 10 (17:00 IST): Ceasefire

The Director General of Military Operations (DGMO) of Pakistan calls his Indian counterpart. An understanding to "stop all firing and military action on land, air, and sea" comes into effect.

The Arsenal

A Tri-Service Doctrine

The operation showcased seamless synergy between the Army, Navy, and Air Force, prominently featuring indigenous 'Atmanirbhar Bharat' platforms.

Aerial Dominance

Made-in-India drones and missiles "exposed vulnerabilities."

  • Drones: TAPAS-BH-201, Rustom
  • Loitering: Nagastra, Warmate
  • Aircraft: LCA Tejas, LCH Prachand

Ground & Air Defence

Indigenous systems provided a multi-layered shield.

  • Missiles: Akash (Stellar Performance)
  • Legacy: Pechora, OSA-AK
  • Artillery: Dhanush, ATAGS

C&C and Naval Power

Network-centric capability and maritime dominance.

  • C&C: Integrated Air Command (IACCS)
  • Intel: 10+ ISRO Satellites
  • Naval: Carrier Battle Group (CBG)
